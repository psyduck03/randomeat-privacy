Privacy Policy — Random Eat

Last updated: 14 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Random Eat app ("the app") handles information. By using the app you agree to this policy.

Data We Collect

The app does not require accounts or sign-in, and does not collect data that personally identifies you.

To keep the app reliable, we use Google Firebase to collect limited, anonymous information:

  • Crash reports (Firebase Crashlytics) — if the app crashes, anonymous diagnostic information (such as device model, operating system version, and the technical cause of the crash) is collected to help us fix the problem.
  • Usage analytics (Firebase Analytics) — anonymous, aggregated information about how the app is used (such as which screens are opened) to help us improve the app.

This information is processed by Google and is not sold or shared with other third parties. For details, see the Firebase Privacy and Security page.

Data Stored on Your Device

Recipes and favorites you create are stored only on your device using local storage. This data is never uploaded to any server. Uninstalling the app removes this data.

Photos

If you add a photo to your own recipe, the app requests permission to access your device's images. The selected photo is stored locally on your device only and is never uploaded or shared.

Internet Access

The app loads meal data and images from public content delivery networks (jsDelivr and Pexels). These requests do not include any personal information about you.

Third-Party Services

Food images are provided by Pexels. Meal data is served via jsDelivr. Crash reporting and analytics are provided by Google Firebase.

Children's Privacy

The app does not knowingly collect any data from children. It is suitable for general audiences.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated occasionally. Changes will be posted on this page.
